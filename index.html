Noomi Basra Tea (شاي نومي بصرة)

Dish Description: A soothing herbal tea made by simmering dried black limes (Noomi Basra). This traditional Iraqi beverage has a tangy taste and is used to aid digestion and relieve cold symptoms.

Ingredients

  • 2 dried black limes (Noomi Basra), lightly cracked
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon honey or sugar (optional)
  • Optional: pinch of cinnamon or crushed cardamom

Instructions

  1. Lightly crack open the dried limes to release flavor.
  2. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a small pot.
  3. Add the limes and optional spices.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 10–15 minutes until the water darkens and becomes aromatic.
  5. Strain into cups and sweeten with honey or sugar if desired.

Iraqi Chai (شاي عراقي)

Dish Description: A rich, dark black tea brewed slowly and traditionally served in small glass cups (istikan). Iraqi Chai is strong, aromatic, and a symbol of hospitality across Iraq.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons loose black tea (Assam or Ceylon)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar (or to taste)
  • Optional: crushed cardamom or fresh mint leaves

Instructions

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a small kettle or saucepan.
  2. Add the tea leaves and simmer gently over low heat for 10–15 minutes.
  3. Strain into small glass cups (istikan).
  4. Sweeten with sugar to taste. Optionally, add cardamom or mint.

Hamad Tea (شاي حمّض)

Dish Description: A tangy and refreshing Iraqi tea made with black tea and lemon juice or dried lime (loomi). Known as "Shai Hamad," this southern Iraqi specialty is enjoyed hot or cold, especially during hot weather or after meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons loose black tea (Assam preferred)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar (or to taste)
  • 2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice or 1 dried lime (loomi), cracked
  • Optional: mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Boil the water in a kettle or saucepan.
  2. Add the tea leaves and dried lime or lemon juice.
  3. Simmer for 10–12 minutes until the tea is strong and infused with the sour flavor.
  4. Strain and pour into small glasses.
  5. Add sugar to taste and optionally garnish with mint.

Bagila Bil Dihin (باقلاء بالدهن)

Dish Description: A classic Iraqi breakfast of broad beans stewed with garlic and layered over Iraqi bread, topped with fried eggs and a drizzle of oil or butter.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked broad beans (or canned)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp cumin (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 pieces Iraqi bread (or pita), torn

Instructions

  1. In a pan, sauté garlic in oil. Add beans, salt, pepper, and cumin. Simmer for 5–10 minutes.
  2. Fry the eggs sunny-side-up in ghee or oil.
  3. Layer bread in a bowl, pour beans over, and top with eggs. Serve hot.

Makhlama (مخلمة)

Dish Description: A savory Iraqi breakfast dish made with seasoned ground beef or lamb, onions, tomatoes, and eggs, served hot with fresh bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Olive oil for frying

Kubbat Burgul (كبة برغل)

Dish Description: Kubbat Burgul is a traditional fried Iraqi dumpling made with a shell of bulgur wheat and semolina, filled with seasoned ground meat and onions. It’s crispy on the outside and tender inside — perfect with salad or yogurt.

Ingredients

  • Shell:
  • 2 cups fine bulgur (soaked 30 min)
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp turmeric (optional)
  • ½ cup water (as needed)

  • Filling:
  • 300g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp allspice or baharat
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley (optional)

  • For Frying: Vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare Shell: Drain soaked bulgur. Mix with semolina, spices, and water until dough is soft and not sticky. Rest 15 minutes.
  2. Prepare Filling: Sauté onion, add meat, spices, and parsley. Cook until browned. Cool slightly.
  3. Form Kubba: Wet hands, flatten dough into discs, stuff with filling, and shape into ovals or torpedoes.
  4. Fry: Heat oil and fry until golden brown and crisp. Drain on paper towels.

Kubbat Halab (كبة حلب)

Dish Description: Kubbat Halab is a traditional Iraqi deep-fried rice dumpling stuffed with seasoned ground meat. Crispy outside and tender inside, it’s often served at feasts and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • For Rice Dough:
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked 1 hour)
  • 4 cups water
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Oil for frying

  • For Filling:
  • 400g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garam masala or allspice
  • 2 tbsp oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare Rice Dough: Boil rice with turmeric and salt until soft and water is absorbed. Let cool, then mash into smooth dough.
  2. Cook Filling: Sauté onion in oil, add meat and spices, cook until browned. Set aside.
  3. Form Kubba: Take a ball of rice dough, flatten, add 1 tbsp filling, seal edges, shape into oval or torpedo.
  4. Fry: Deep-fry in hot oil at 175°C (350°F) until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

Kubbat Potata (كبة بطاطا)

Dish Description: Kubbat Potata is a beloved Iraqi dish made from mashed potatoes filled with spiced ground meat and onions, shaped into patties, and fried to golden perfection. A soft, comforting meal often served with salad or pickles.

Ingredients

  • Potato Shell:
  • 5 medium potatoes, peeled and boiled
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp flour or breadcrumbs
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

  • Filling:
  • 300g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp allspice or baharat
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley (optional)
  • 2 tbsp oil

  • For Frying: Vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the shell: Mash the boiled potatoes while warm. Add egg, flour, salt, and pepper. Mix until a smooth, soft dough forms. Set aside to cool.
  2. Prepare filling: Sauté onion in oil until soft. Add ground meat, spices, and cook until browned. Stir in parsley. Let cool.
  3. Shape Kubba: With oiled hands, take a small ball of potato mix, flatten into a disc, add 1 tbsp filling, and close into a patty or oval.
  4. Fry: Heat oil in a pan and fry each kubba until golden on both sides. Drain on paper towels.

Kubbat Hamuth (كبة حامض)

Dish Description: A classic Iraqi sour soup made with kubba (meat-filled dumplings), Swiss chard, turnips, and lemon. It’s hearty, healthy, and deeply comforting.

Ingredients

  • Kubba Shell: 1½ cups rice flour, 250g ground beef, salt, water
  • Filling: 250g ground beef, 1 small onion (minced), ½ tsp allspice, ¼ tsp cinnamon, salt, pepper
  • Soup Base:
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 turnips, peeled and diced
  • 4 Swiss chard leaves, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp crushed coriander
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (optional)
  • ¼ cup lemon juice (or to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 5 cups water or vegetable broth

Instructions

  1. Make Kubba Dough: Mix rice flour, salt, and beef. Add water slowly to form smooth dough. Chill briefly.
  2. Prepare Filling: Sauté beef and onion with spices until fully cooked.
  3. Shape Kubba: Form dough into balls, flatten, fill with meat mixture, seal and roll smooth.
  4. Cook Soup: Sauté onions in oil. Add turnips, chard, turmeric, coriander, and optional tomato paste. Cook 5 minutes.
  5. Add lemon juice, salt, pepper, and broth. Simmer 10 minutes.
  6. Add kubba balls carefully. Simmer 25–30 minutes until cooked through.
  7. Adjust seasoning and serve hot.

Kubbat Jerish (كبة جريش)

Dish Description: Kubbat Jerish is a comforting Iraqi dish made with a shell of cracked wheat (jerish) and semolina, stuffed with spiced ground meat, and gently cooked in a lemony tomato broth. Soft, tangy, and savory — it's a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • Shell:
  • 2 cups fine bulgur (jerish)
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Water (as needed to knead)

  • Filling:
  • 400g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp allspice
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oil

  • Broth:
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare Shell: Mix jerish, semolina, salt, turmeric, and pepper. Add water slowly to form smooth, pliable dough. Rest 20 mins covered.
  2. Make Filling: Sauté onion in oil, add meat and spices. Cook till browned. Stir in parsley and let cool.
  3. Shape Kubba: Form small balls of dough, flatten, fill with meat, and seal into oval shapes.
  4. Make Broth: Sauté onion in oil. Add tomato paste, salt, turmeric, and water. Bring to boil, then add lemon juice.
  5. Cook: Gently add kubba to simmering broth. Cook 30–35 mins on low until tender.

Mujadara (مجدره عراقية)

Dish Description: Mujadara is a humble yet hearty vegetarian Iraqi dish made with lentils, rice, and sweet caramelized onions. It’s nutritious, affordable, and packed with flavor — a favorite comfort food throughout the Levant and Iraq.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup brown or green lentils
  • 3/4 cup rice (preferably basmati)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil (divided)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth

Instructions

  1. In a pot, boil the lentils in 2 cups of water for 15 minutes until halfway cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a deep pan, heat 2 tbsp oil and sauté the onions on low-medium heat until deeply browned and caramelized (20–25 minutes). Remove half the onions for garnish.
  3. Add the remaining oil, rice, lentils, cumin, salt, pepper, and 2 more cups of water or broth. Stir well.
  4. Bring to a boil, then lower heat, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes or until rice and lentils are fully cooked.
  5. Fluff with a fork and top with the reserved caramelized onions.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature with salad or yogurt.

Timman Jizar (تمن جزر)

Dish Description: A colorful and lightly sweet Iraqi rice dish made with shredded carrots, basmati rice, onions, and warm spices like cinnamon and cardamom. Often served with meat or on its own as a vegetarian main.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice (rinsed and soaked for 20 minutes)
  • 2 large carrots, grated
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • Optional: toasted almonds or raisins for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat oil or ghee and sauté the chopped onion until soft and golden.
  2. Add grated carrots and cook for 3–5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in cinnamon, cardamom, pepper, and salt.
  4. Add the soaked rice and stir gently to coat with the spices and carrots.
  5. Pour in water or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15–20 minutes until rice is fluffy.
  6. Let sit for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork. Garnish with nuts or raisins if desired.

Margat Bagla (مرقة باقلة)

Dish Description: Margat Bagla is a flavorful Iraqi stew made with fava beans, tomato, and spices. It can be made vegetarian or with tender meat cubes, and is commonly served with rice or Iraqi bread. It’s rich in fiber, protein, and comforting flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen fava beans (peeled)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 large tomato, grated or finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water or vegetable/meat broth
  • Optional: 250g lamb or beef cubes

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot and sauté the onions until soft and golden.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. (Optional) Add meat cubes and brown them on all sides.
  4. Add tomato paste, grated tomato, turmeric, cumin, black pepper, and salt. Stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Add the fava beans and mix to coat them in the sauce.
  6. Pour in water or broth and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 30–40 minutes (longer if using meat) until the beans are soft and the stew thickens.
  7. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with white rice or warm khubz (flatbread).

Iraqi Grilled Fish in Oven with Sauce (سمك مشوي بالفرن مع الصلصة)

Dish Description: This Iraqi-style oven-baked fish is marinated with spices and lemon, then grilled or baked in the oven and topped with a rich tomato, garlic, and pepper sauce. It's a hearty, flavorful main dish typically served with rice or khubz (Iraqi flatbread).

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish (zubaidi, carp, or seabass), cleaned and scored
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced into rings
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup hot water

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (390°F).
  2. Mix turmeric, cumin, black pepper, salt, lemon juice, and olive oil. Rub the fish with this marinade inside and out. Let rest for 15–30 minutes.
  3. In a baking dish, layer onions, bell peppers, and tomato slices. Place the fish on top.
  4. In a pan, heat olive oil. Add garlic, grated tomato, tomato paste, cumin, salt, pepper, and water. Simmer for 5–7 minutes until sauce thickens.
  5. Pour sauce over the fish and vegetables.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 30–35 minutes. Uncover and broil for 5–10 minutes until golden.
  7. Serve hot with rice or warm Iraqi bread.

Fried Zubaidi Fish (زبيدي مقلي)

Dish Description: Zubaidi fish, also known as silver pomfret, is a prized delicacy in Iraq and the Gulf. Light, flaky, and mild in flavor, it’s typically marinated with spices and lemon juice, then deep-fried to golden perfection. It’s often served with rice, salad, or flatbread.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole Zubaidi fish (cleaned and scored)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Oil for deep or shallow frying
  • Optional: fresh coriander and lemon slices for garnish

Instructions

  1. Clean and pat dry the Zubaidi fish. Score the sides for better flavor absorption.
  2. Rub the fish with turmeric, cumin, pepper, salt, and lemon juice. Let it marinate for 20–30 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Carefully place the fish into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and cooked through (about 5–7 minutes per side).
  5. Remove and drain on paper towels. Garnish with coriander and lemon slices.
  6. Serve hot with basmati rice, Iraqi salad, or khubz (flatbread).

Samak Mutabbaq (مطبق سمك)

Dish Description: Samak Mutabbaq is a signature southern Iraqi dish from Basra made with layers of fried fish, tomato-onion sauce, and fragrant basmati rice. The name "mutabbaq" means "layered" — and this dish is just that: comforting, flavorful, and perfect for family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish or 500g fillets (carp, seabass, or similar)
  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander or parsley
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Water or fish broth as needed
  • Optional: lemon slices or dried lime (loomi)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Fish: Clean and season the fish with turmeric, salt, and pepper. Pan-fry until golden. Set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a pot, heat oil and cook onions until golden. Add garlic, tomato paste, tomatoes, and cook until thickened.
  3. Spice it Up: Add turmeric, cumin, black pepper, salt, and optional loomi. Mix in chopped herbs.
  4. Layer the Dish:
    • Spread half the tomato mixture at the bottom of the pot.
    • Place fish pieces on top.
    • Cover with remaining sauce.
    • Add soaked rice on top.
  5. Cook: Add hot water or broth to just cover the rice. Cover and cook over low heat for 30–40 minutes until rice is done.
  6. Serve: Flip onto a serving dish (like maqluba) or serve as layered rice and fish. Garnish with lemon or parsley.

Khubz ‘Aroog (خبيز عروق)

Dish Description: Khubz ‘Aroog is a flavorful Iraqi flatbread made by mixing ground meat with herbs, onions, spices, and eggs, then pan-frying or baking the mixture like a thick pancake. Crispy outside, soft inside — it's a savory treat enjoyed during Ramadan, gatherings, or as a quick hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup green onions or leeks, chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely diced (optional)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose or chickpea flour
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying or baking

Instructions

  1. Mix meat, onion, garlic, parsley, green onions, and tomato in a large bowl.
  2. Add eggs, flour, and all spices. Stir until it becomes a thick, sticky batter.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Spread a ladle of the mixture into the pan and flatten into a thick circle.
  5. Cook 4–5 minutes per side until golden and cooked through.
  6. Repeat with remaining mixture, or bake in a tray at 180°C (350°F) for 25–30 minutes.
  7. Slice and serve warm with yogurt or salad.

Torshana (طرشانة)

Dish Description: A traditional Iraqi sweet-and-sour stew made with tender lamb or beef, pomegranate molasses, and dried fruits like apricots, prunes, and raisins. Often served during special occasions with white rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g lamb or beef, cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ghee or olive oil
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup dried apricots
  • 1/4 cup prunes
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • 2 tbsp slivered almonds (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water (as needed to cover meat)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pot. Sauté the chopped onions until soft.
  2. Add the meat and brown well on all sides.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Add enough water to cover.
  4. Simmer covered for 45–60 minutes until the meat is tender.
  5. Add dried fruits, sugar, and pomegranate molasses. Stir gently.
  6. Simmer uncovered for another 20–30 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. Garnish with almonds if desired and serve hot with rice.

Shabzi (شابزي)

Dish Description: Shabzi is a rich and aromatic Iraqi herb stew made with fresh greens like parsley, cilantro, and spinach, combined with tender beef or lamb and dried limes for a tangy depth. It's a cousin of Persian Ghormeh Sabzi and is often served with steaming white rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef or lamb, cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup leek or green onion tops (optional)
  • 2 dried limes (loomi), pierced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (optional)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water or beef broth as needed

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté the onion until golden.
  2. Add the meat, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Brown the meat on all sides.
  3. Add tomato paste (if using), cover with water or broth, and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add pierced dried limes and let stew for 45–60 minutes until meat is tender.
  5. Meanwhile, lightly sauté all greens in a separate pan until wilted and aromatic.
  6. Add the sautéed greens to the meat and simmer together for another 30 minutes uncovered.
  7. Adjust seasoning and serve with steamed basmati rice.

Tahini Dessert (Halawat Tahiniyah – حلاوة الطحينية)

Dish Description: A rich and naturally sweet Middle Eastern dessert made with tahini, maple syrup or honey, and ground nuts. This no-bake treat is soft, satisfying, and perfect for a quick, healthy dessert. It’s vegan, gluten-free, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup finely ground nuts (walnuts, pistachios, almonds)
  • 1/2 cup oat flour or almond flour (or powdered milk for richer version)
  • Optional: cinnamon, cardamom, or sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  3. Shape into balls, press into molds, or flatten into a small dish or tray.
  4. Garnish with cinnamon, cardamom, or sesame seeds if desired.
  5. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes if a firmer texture is preferred.
  6. Store in an airtight container and serve at room temperature or cold.

Halawa Halib (حلاوة حليب)

Dish Description: Halawa Halib is a beloved Iraqi dessert made with powdered milk, flour, and sugar syrup. It’s rich, creamy, and often served on special occasions or as a comforting homemade sweet.

  • 1 cup powdered milk
  • 1 cup corn or sunflower oil

  • Sugar Syrup:
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 2 cups water

Halawa Tamar (حلاوة تمر)

Dish Description: Halawa Tamar is a classic Iraqi dessert made from soft dates mixed with ghee and sweetened milk, often shaped into balls and rolled in sesame or nuts. It's a nutritious and naturally sweet treat enjoyed during Ramadan or festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • 200g soft, pitted dates
  • 1 tbsp butter or ghee
  • 3 tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup tahini (optional filling)
  • 1/2 cup toasted sesame seeds or crushed pistachios (for coating)

Instructions

  1. In a non-stick pan, melt the ghee over medium heat.
  2. Add the dates and mash with a spoon, cooking for 5–7 minutes until soft and pasty.
  3. Pour in the condensed milk and stir well to combine. Cook another 2–3 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Let the mixture cool slightly. With greased hands, roll into small balls.
  5. (Optional) Make a dent in each ball, spoon in a bit of tahini, then seal and roll again.
  6. Coat each ball with sesame seeds or pistachios.
  7. Arrange on a tray or in mini paper cups. Serve or store in an airtight container.

Zalabia (زلابية عراقية)

Dish Description: Zalabia is a traditional Iraqi deep-fried dessert made from fermented flour batter shaped into spirals, then soaked in fragrant sugar syrup. It’s crunchy, sweet, and served during Ramadan and festive gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Batter:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2½ cups warm water
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

  • Syrup:
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 1½ cups water
  • ¼ tsp citric acid or 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp orange blossom water

Instructions

  1. Prepare Syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and citric acid. Bring to a boil, simmer 10–12 mins until thickened. Stir in orange blossom water and cool completely.
  2. Make Batter: In a large bowl, mix flour, cornstarch, yeast, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add water and whisk to a smooth batter. Let rest 2 hours.
  3. Fry: Heat oil in deep pan. Pour batter into squeeze bottle or piping bag. Squeeze into hot oil in spiral shapes (3-inch rounds). Fry until firm but not golden.
  4. Soak: Remove from oil and immediately dip into cold syrup. Coat well, then transfer to serving plate.
  5. Best served fresh and crisp!
