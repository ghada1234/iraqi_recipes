Healthy Makhlama (مخلمة صحي)

Dish Description: A healthier take on the traditional Iraqi skillet of meat and eggs, using lean ground meat, extra vegetables, and minimal olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 300g lean ground beef or turkey
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, diced (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2–3 eggs (poached or boiled instead of fried)
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Serve with whole grain or low-carb bread

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a nonstick skillet and sauté onions until soft.
  2. Add lean ground meat and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat if needed.
  3. Add tomatoes, zucchini, and spices. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Poach eggs separately and place them on top before serving.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with healthy bread.

Healthy Kahi with Geymar (كاهي و قيمر صحية)

Dish Description: A lighter version of the beloved Iraqi breakfast using baked whole wheat pastry and homemade low-fat geymar made with yogurt and milk. Served with natural date syrup.

Ingredients

  • For Healthy Kahi:
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/3 cup warm water
  • For Low-Fat Geymar:
  • 1 cup low-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • Optional: rosewater
  • Natural date syrup for serving

Instructions

  1. Kahi: Mix dough ingredients and knead well. Roll into thin sheets, fold, and bake at 200°C (390°F) for 10–12 mins until golden.
  2. Geymar: Simmer milk, then let cool slightly. Stir in yogurt and let thicken in the fridge for 6–8 hours.
  3. Serve warm kahi with chilled geymar and drizzle of date syrup.

Healthy Bagila Bil Dihin (باقلاء بالدهن صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter version of the traditional Iraqi breakfast, using minimal olive oil, boiled eggs, and whole grain bread for a nourishing start to the day.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked broad beans (or canned, rinsed)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 boiled eggs (instead of fried)
  • 2 pieces whole wheat pita or Iraqi whole grain bread
  • Fresh parsley or mint (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a nonstick pan, lightly sauté garlic in 1 tsp olive oil.
  2. Add beans, cumin, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 5–10 minutes.
  3. Boil eggs separately and slice in half.
  4. Layer whole wheat bread in a bowl, pour beans over, and top with egg halves.
  5. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve warm.

Healthy Torshana (طرشانة صحية)

Dish Description: A lighter, healthier twist on the traditional Iraqi sweet-and-sour stew. This version uses lean chicken or beef, skips added sugars, and includes vegetables for a nutrient-packed meal that keeps the authentic flavor intact.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken breast (cubed) or lean beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1/4 cup dried apricots (unsweetened)
  • 1/4 cup prunes
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • 1 small carrot, diced (optional)
  • 1 zucchini, diced (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water or low-sodium broth (as needed)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a nonstick pot and sauté the onion until translucent.
  2. Add chicken (or lean beef) and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Add water or broth to cover.
  4. Simmer 30–40 minutes for chicken or 60 minutes for beef until tender.
  5. Add dried fruits and optional vegetables. Simmer uncovered for 15–20 more minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Stir in pomegranate molasses near the end. Adjust seasoning.
  7. Garnish with parsley and serve hot over brown rice or cauliflower rice.

Healthy Quzi (قوزي صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter version using lean lamb cuts, brown rice, and roasted nuts without frying.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups brown basmati rice
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup almonds, toasted

Instructions

Healthy Tepsi Baytinijan (تبسي باذنجان صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter version of the classic casserole, with baked eggplant, lean ground meat, and less oil. Still rich in flavor and perfect for a wholesome lunch.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggplants, sliced and baked (not fried)
  • 300g lean ground beef or turkey
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, sliced and baked
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt, pepper, cumin, and paprika to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (low sodium)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (390°F).
  2. Lightly brush eggplant and potato slices with olive oil and bake until tender.
  3. Sauté onion and garlic, then add lean meat and spices until cooked.
  4. In a baking dish, layer vegetables and meat. Pour tomato sauce over.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 25–30 minutes.

Healthy Maqluba (مقلوبة صحية)

Dish Description: A nutritious twist on the traditional layered rice dish, using skinless chicken, baked vegetables, and brown rice for a fiber-rich, heart-friendly meal.

Ingredients

  • 500g skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 2 cups brown basmati rice (soaked)
  • 2 eggplants, 2 potatoes, 2 tomatoes (baked, not fried)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bake eggplant, potato, and tomato slices until soft and golden.
  2. Lightly sauté onions and chicken with spices in olive oil or water.
  3. In a pot, layer vegetables, chicken, and soaked brown rice.
  4. Pour broth on top, cover tightly, and simmer on low for 45–50 minutes.
  5. Flip the pot upside down onto a platter to serve.

Healthy Dolma (دولمة صحية)

Dish Description: A lighter take on the traditional Iraqi dolma, made with lean ground meat, less oil, and more herbs. Vegetables are stuffed and simmered without lamb layers for a lower-fat, high-fiber option.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups brown rice
  • 500g lean ground beef or turkey
  • Chopped parsley, dill, and mint (1/2 cup total)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • Vegetables for stuffing: bell peppers, zucchini, vine leaves, eggplant
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, cumin, and allspice

Instructions

  1. Mix brown rice, ground meat, herbs, onion, tomato paste, and spices.
  2. Stuff vegetables and layer tightly in a deep pot.
  3. Drizzle lemon juice and olive oil on top. Add water just to cover.
  4. Cover with a plate and lid. Simmer for 1–1.5 hours until vegetables are tender.

Healthy Masgouf (مسگوف صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter take on the iconic Iraqi grilled carp, using less oil and baked instead of grilled. The fish is marinated with fresh herbs, garlic, lemon juice, and a touch of tamarind.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole carp (butterflied and cleaned)
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley and dill for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (390°F).
  2. Mix lemon juice, tamarind, garlic, turmeric, cumin, and olive oil into a marinade.
  3. Rub the fish inside and out with the marinade and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Place on a baking tray lined with parchment paper and bake for 25–30 minutes until flaky and browned.
  5. Garnish with herbs and serve with a fresh salad or brown rice.

Healthy Tashreeb Laham (تشريب لحم صحي)

Dish Description: A leaner and lighter version of the traditional lamb stew, using trimmed lamb, baked whole-wheat flatbread, and less oil while preserving all the comforting flavors.

Ingredients

  • 400g lean lamb (trimmed of fat)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (low-sodium)
  • 5 cups water or vegetable broth (low-sodium)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • 2 small whole-wheat flatbreads, toasted and torn

Instructions

  1. In a pot, sauté onion and garlic in 1 tsp olive oil until soft.
  2. Add the lamb and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add tomatoes, tomato paste, spices, and broth.
  4. Simmer for about 1 hour until lamb is tender.
  5. Layer whole-wheat bread in a bowl and pour the stew on top.
  6. Let it soak, then serve with a side of fresh parsley or salad.

Healthy Kubba Mosul (كبة موصل صحي)

Dish Description: A healthier take on the traditional Mosul meat pie, using lean ground meat, bulgur, and baking instead of frying, while maintaining the authentic flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fine bulgur
  • 400g lean ground beef or lamb (for dough)
  • 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper
  • Filling:
  • 300g lean ground meat
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (instead of pine nuts)
  • 1 tsp allspice, 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spray olive oil (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. Soak bulgur in warm water until soft. Mix with ground meat and seasonings to form a dough.
  2. Sauté onion and meat with spices for the filling. Stir in walnuts.
  3. Press half of dough in a nonstick tray, spread filling, then top with remaining dough layer.
  4. Score into wedges, spray lightly with olive oil, and bake at 200°C (400°F) for 25–30 minutes until crisp and golden.

Healthy Timman Bagilla (تمن باقلة صحي)

Dish Description: A nutritious take on the traditional fava bean and rice dish using brown basmati rice, less oil, and boiled eggs for protein.

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen fava beans
  • 1 bunch fresh dill (or 2 tbsp dried dill)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 boiled eggs, sliced (for topping)

  2. In a pot, lightly sauté dill and fava beans in olive oil.

  4. Top with sliced boiled eggs before serving.

Healthy Tashreeb Dajaj (تشريب دجاج صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter variation of the traditional chicken and chickpea stew using skinless chicken breast, minimal oil, and whole wheat flatbread.

Ingredients

  • 600g skinless chicken breast, cut into chunks
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (low-sodium)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 dried limes (noomi basra), pierced
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup canned low-sodium chickpeas
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 whole wheat flatbread, torn into pieces
  • Spices: cumin, turmeric, black pepper

Instructions

  1. In a pot, sauté onions and garlic in olive oil.
  2. Add chicken pieces and brown lightly.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and pureed tomatoes, add spices and dried limes.
  4. Pour in broth and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Add chickpeas and cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Layer whole wheat bread in a bowl and pour stew on top.

Healthy Qeema (قيمة نجفية صحية)

Dish Description: A leaner, more nutritious version of the classic Qeema stew from Najaf using ground turkey or lean beef, high-fiber chickpeas, and less oil and salt.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground turkey or 90% lean ground beef
  • 1 cup yellow split chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water or low-sodium broth
  • Olive oil spray (or 1 tsp)

Instructions

  1. Sauté onions with a light spray of olive oil until golden.
  2. Add ground turkey or beef, cook until browned.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and spices. Add chickpeas and water.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 45–60 mins until thick and flavorful.
  5. Serve with brown rice or whole wheat flatbread.

Healthy Pacha (پاچة صحية)

Healthy Margat Bamia (مرقة بامية صحية)

Dish Description: A nutritious version of the classic okra stew, made with lean meat, minimal oil, and rich tomato flavor — all without sacrificing taste.

Ingredients

  • 400g lean lamb or skinless chicken breast
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen okra
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cumin (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable or meat broth

Instructions

  1. In a nonstick pot, heat olive oil and sauté onions and garlic until soft.
  2. Add meat and cook until browned. Stir in tomatoes and tomato paste.
  3. Add turmeric, cumin, salt, pepper, and broth. Simmer for 30–40 minutes.
  4. Add okra and cook another 15–20 minutes until tender. Serve over brown rice.

Healthy Iraqi Kebab (كباب عراقي صحي)

Dish Description: A nutritious twist on the traditional Iraqi kebab using lean ground meat, herbs, and oven-baking instead of grilling. Lower in fat and sodium while preserving the signature flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g lean ground beef or turkey (90–95% lean)
  • 1 small onion, finely grated and squeezed
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil spray (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (390°F). Line a tray with parchment paper.
  2. Mix all ingredients well by hand until the mixture is sticky.
  3. Shape into log-style kebabs or patties. Place on tray.
  4. Lightly spray with olive oil and bake for 15–20 minutes, flipping halfway.
  5. Serve with whole wheat bread, grilled tomatoes, and sumac onions.

Healthy Halawat Sha’riyya (حلاوة شعرية صحية)

  • 1 tbsp olive oil or light ghee
  • 1/4 cup honey or coconut sugar

Healthy Zarda (زردة صحية)

Dish Description: A healthier twist on the classic sweet yellow rice, made with brown rice, reduced sugar, and no added butter. Light, fragrant, and festive.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup brown basmati rice
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar or unrefined cane sugar
  • 1/4 tsp saffron threads
  • 1 tbsp rosewater
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional)
  • 1 tbsp raisins
  • 1 tbsp chopped almonds or pistachios

Instructions

  1. Soak rice for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Boil rice until 70% cooked, then drain again.
  3. In a pot, mix rice with saffron, sugar, rosewater, cardamom, and olive oil.
  4. Steam on low heat for 15 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Top with raisins and nuts before serving.

Healthy Kleicha (كليجة صحي)

Dish Description: A lighter version of the traditional Iraqi date-filled cookie, made with whole wheat flour, less sugar, and coconut oil instead of ghee.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil (or light olive oil)
  • 1/4 cup low-fat milk or plant milk
  • 2 tbsp honey or date syrup
  • 1 tsp yeast
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • Filling: 3/4 cup soft date paste + 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, yeast, cardamom, oil, and milk to form a soft dough. Let it rise for 1 hour.
  2. Roll out dough and spread date filling. Roll into a log and slice or shape into patterns.
  3. Place on a baking tray and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15–20 minutes until golden.
